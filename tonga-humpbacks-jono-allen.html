About the Experience
An expedition, not a tour

Join Jono and a small group of ocean-loving travellers in the remote island group of Vava'u, Kingdom of Tonga, where humpback whales return each year to mate, calve, and raise their young in warm, crystal-clear waters.

Each day you'll board a private vessel with expert guides and experienced skippers to find and swim alongside these extraordinary animals. This isn't a crowded whale-watching trip where you glimpse a tail from a distance. You'll be in the water, face-to-face, watching a mother and calf pass beneath you.

Between whale encounters, you'll drop anchor over vibrant coral reefs, explore blue caves and deserted beaches, and get to know the landscape both above and below the surface. Evenings are spent back at your ocean-view accommodation sharing stories over meals prepared by the resort's award-winning chef.

This trip is for anyone who feels at home in the water. Whether you're a photographer, freediver, or simply someone who's always dreamed of swimming with whales, the only prerequisite is that you're a confident snorkeller.

Your Storyteller Guide
Jono Allen

Environmental scientist, marine megafauna guide, and ocean cinematographer. Jono's knowledge and years in the water get you as close as possible to these creatures, creating encounters most people only dream about.

This isn't a tourist whale-watching tour. Jono rejects the "smile-for-the-camera" approach. He guides you on a journey of exploration and understanding. You'll learn about humpback physiology, behaviour, and individual personalities. Every encounter is immersive, enlightening, and deeply personal.

Jono is also committed to the local Tongan community of Vava'u. By working with local services, staff, cuisine, and accommodations, your experience maintains authenticity while directly supporting the people and place that make it all possible.

Why Book This Experience?
Beyond a whale tour

Most whale tours in Tonga give guests a single brief swim, share the water with large groups, and rush through the day. One encounter isn't enough to truly connect with these animals.

This trip is built differently. Five full days on the water with the same small group, learning to read whale behaviour, moving calmly and respectfully in the water, and letting genuine encounters unfold naturally rather than chasing the next tick-box moment.

With private charters and a maximum of seven guests, you get time and space in the water that a public tour simply can't offer. For photographers, it's ideal: Jono provides hands-on guidance throughout the trip, from shooting technique to editing your best frames on the final day.

By slowing the pace and keeping the experience personal, this week-long expedition removes the rush of a one-day tour and lets you truly immerse yourself in Tonga's incredible marine world.

Each day you board a private vessel operated by one of the most experienced whale-watching teams in Tonga. With a maximum of seven guests, you have plenty of space on board and in the water.
